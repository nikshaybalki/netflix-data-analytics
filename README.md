# netflix-data-analytics

Project Insights: The Story of the Data
By using Pandas to clean an uncurated dataset of over 8,800 entries and Matplotlib to plot the results, I uncovered the underlying business trends driving Netflix's platform strategy:

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Findings
Catalog Composition (Bar Chart): Despite the cultural rise of binge-worthy series, standalone Movies significantly outnumber TV Shows, remaining the core pillar of Netflix's inventory structure.

Demographic Targeting (Pie Chart): The library heavily favors TV-MA and TV-14 ratings over family-friendly content, proving a strategic focus on mature and young-adult viewership segments.

Geographical Density (Horizontal Bar Chart): The United States sits uncontested as the primary content producer, while India and the United Kingdom firmly hold the secondary tier of dense media generation.

Continuous Feature Distribution (Histogram): Movie runtimes sharply normalize and peak at the 90-to-100-minute mark, indicating calculated tailoring to modern consumer attention spans.

Temporal Cluster Analysis (Scatter Plot): Plotting timelines reveals an exponential content spike over the last decade, visually capturing the exact era Netflix shifted from a licensing platform to an original production powerhouse.
